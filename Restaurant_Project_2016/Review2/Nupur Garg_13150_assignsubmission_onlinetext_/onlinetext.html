REVIEWER: Nupur Garg
NAME: Firestone Grill
ADDRESS: 1001 Higuera St Suite A
CITY: San Luis Obispo
FOOD: 2
SERVICE: 4
VENUE: 3
RATING: 3
WRITTEN REVIEW:

Firestone Grill is a popular barbecue and American food restaurant. As a vegetarian, I have never had an interest to go to Firestone Grill. Walking up the restaurant, I saw plenty of outdoor seating. It appeared to be somewhere in between a fast food restaurant and a sit in restaurant. Upon walking inside, I was slightly disappointed to note there was no A.C. inside the building, given that it was a hot day outside.


I ordered a Veggie Avocado sandwich. As someone who has tried most other sandwich places in San Luis Obispo, I was pleasantly surprised with the price of the sandwich. It was only $6 compared to $10 sandwiches at most other places. However, upon eating the sandwich, I was less excited. The sandwich ended up being something I could easily make myself. The sandwich contained avocado, lettuce, tomato, onion, sprouts and jack cheese on toasted wheat bread. I was glad the bread was toasted - giving the sandwich a slight crunch. However, the bread itself was a generic bread and did not provide much flavor. Similarly, the rest of the sandwich did not have a standout aspect to it. The sauce did provide some flavor, however, not enough to make the vegetables stand out. Although I could see some people enjoying the flavors in the sandwich, I prefer bold and complex flavors and was hoping for the jack cheese to be more prominent and tie the sandwich together. It felt like this sandwich felt short of its goal to use avocado as a replacement for meat.


The service was order and pick up at the counter. The line was not very long and moved quickly. The people at the counter ordering my food were extremely polite. The sandwich came out quicker than I expected making the whole process smooth. The ambiance itself had some nice music and had some sports games playing in the background. It was quiet enough to have a conversation with a friend without having to speak too loudly.


Overall, although the restaurant was on the cheaper side, I would rather spend a few more dollars on a restaurant that has more flavorful options for vegetarians. I did enjoy the environment, so I could see myself coming with friends mostly to enjoy my friendsâ€™ company, instead of for the food.
