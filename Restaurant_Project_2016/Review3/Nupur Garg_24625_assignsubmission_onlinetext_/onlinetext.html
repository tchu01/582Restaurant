REVIEWER: Nupur Garg

NAME: Araya’s Place
ADDRESS: 10246 Main St c
CITY: Bellevue
FOOD: 5
SERVICE: 4
VENUE: 5
RATING: 5
WRITTEN REVIEW:

Araya’s Place is a vegan thai restaurant and bar in Bellevue. Being vegetarian, I was excited for going to a vegan thai place, but my friends who eat meat were a little worried it would be too far out of their wheelhouse. Walking towards the restaurant, the outside is white and wooden and contains character. Entering the restaurant, the ambiance inside the restaurant immediately feels pleasant. The tables are granite looking table tops with wooden stitched chairs. It reminds me of how a country club might look mixed in with Thai decor. There is greenery all over including plants hanging from the ceiling. There are wine bottles lined up on the walls and plates stacked on the bar. Two of the waitresses are wearing jerseys to support the Seahawks game that took place earlier in the day.


Between my friends and I, we decided to try a variety of items at the restaurant. We ordered mango juice, pot stickers, and drunken mushrooms. Overall, the menu was more expensive than I would have liked with the pot stickers around $8 and drunken mushroom around $14. Water is served in wine bottles with a reusable stopper. The mango juice came out first and was rich and flavorful, and had a small umbrella as decor. The potstickers came next. They had a different filling than I was used to, including celery, tofu and carrots. They had a good ratio of skin to filling and were steamed giving the skin had the perfect texture. The drunken mushrooms - which I got at 4 out of 5 spiciness level - was made of wide rice noodles in garlic and chili sauce with asparagus, portobello, shiitake and white mushrooms topped with basil. The dish was huge in proportion and easily could be used for two meals. Although the dish was not as spicy as I would have expected, the sauce was not lacking in flavor. Each of the three mushrooms could be differentiated and had a significantly different texture and taste that assisted the taste of the noodles. Overall, the dish was one I would order again.


The service was nothing spectacular but was not bad either. We were seated quickly and asked for our order promptly as well. Initially they missed the rice from a friend’s dish. However, upon request the rice came out quickly. They refilled the water once through our meal. No extra time was wasted in giving us our checks and packing our meals to go.


Overall, the restaurant was quaint, had a good environment which I would go to again. The food, although on the more expensive side, could be used for multiple meals. All the foods I tried had an incredible amount of flavor. Additionally, as a vegetarian I would have a huge array of options to try every time I go back. If I ever go to Seattle again, I will definitely be coming back.
