REVIEWER: Jon Doughty

NAME: Petra

ADDRESS: 1210 Higuera Street

CITY: San Luis Obispo

FOOD: 4

SERVICE: 4

VENUE: 4

RATING: 4 

WRITTEN REVIEW:

Petra is a fun little restaurant located south of campus. Its situated in a

single story building on the corner of Higuera and Toro street.  The parking

lot is small, but there was some street parking available. 


The inside of the restaurant is rectangular shaped and has maybe a dozen

tables. There are also a few tables right outside. Upon entering, you can order

at the counter directly in front of you. One thing that caught my eye was a

large selection of drinks located to the right after coming through the front

door. I checked out the selection, and it consisted of a variety of drinks, but

mainly craft beers. They had a small selection of six-packs, but a good

selection of 22oz bombers. I had not tried many of the beers there, so I went

with a Ninkasi Tricerahops which is a double IPA.


Back at the front counter, the menu choices are all above along with some

desserts and a few beers on tap. I decided to go with the Gyro Plate. It came

with a mixture of lamb and beef slices, some romaine lettuce with tomatoes,

hummus, a yogurt sauce, and pita bread. Both the meats were seasoned and tasted

delicious, although not particularly tender, they were very flavorful.  The

sides were all good as well, the tomatoes and salad were fresh and the pita was

still warm. I really liked the hummus and the yogurt sauce as well. They also

had a little bar with more sauces. I had some of the red one, although not sure

what it was, it had a good spice to it.


The staff at Petra were all friendly and seemed to be in a good mood. They were

happy to answer my questions about various dishes. I ordered my food to-go and

they brought it out to me when it was ready. I ended up barely finishing the

whole meal, it was much more food than I had originally anticipated. For a

quick, fairly cheap meal, Petra is a great go-to spot and I plan to visit

again.
