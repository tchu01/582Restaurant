REVIEWER: Nupur Garg

NAME: SLO Brew
ADDRESS: 736 Higuera St
CITY: San Luis Obispo
FOOD: 4
SERVICE: 3
VENUE: 4
RATING: 4
WRITTEN REVIEW:

SLO Brew recently reopened after significant renovation. It is mostly known for being a brewerie however also serves American food including pizza, sandwiches, and salads for lunch and dinner. As you walk in, you walk by an open sliding glass wall opened up to outside seating. Looking across the restaurant, SLO Brew appears to be massive. In addition to its tall ceilings it extends far to the other side where there is additional outdoor patio seating. Inside there are a variety of tables including large party booths, raised tables, and shorter smaller tables.


Being a vegetarian there were not too many options available to me on the menu. I selected the daily grilled cheese with a side salad. The side salad comprised solely of greens with oil based dressing. Although the dressing did liven up the salad a little, the greens itself tasted slightly bitter and were not the most pleasurable. The grilled cheese on the other hand was a pleasant surprise. The mesh of cheeses with the jam on the charred bread was the perfect sweetness and contained a lovely crunch. Although the cheese alone was not much to make my palate excited, the sandwich as a whole was a perfect level of sweet without having an aftertaste of having eaten a sugary meal. The meal cost $12 and rounded off to $15 with tax and tip, so it was relatively pricey. However, with the cheese and bread being filling, I was able to take home half the sandwich for a later meal, making it a more reasonable option.


The service was nothing spectacular but was not lacking either. We were greeted at the door and were immediately helped by a waitress who quickly took our order. During the meal we were asked a few times how everything was going and if we needed anything else. The restaurant got progressively busier as we were eating. However, the environment overall stayed peaceful and relatively quiet. The music was quiet enough that you could talk but loud enough that it provided a nice background noise.


Overall, the environment was one that would make me want to visit SLO Brew again if my friends were going. Although I would not go too often given the price, it could be something I would go to once every few months. Additionally, although I enjoyed my experience, there were not too many meals for me to get as a vegetarian so I would have to repeat meals if I went too often. For those reasons, I would recommend it to people but it will not be added to my list of restaurants I go to regularly.
