REVIEWER: Gavin Scott

NAME: Oki Momo

ADDRESS: 2256 Broad Street, #120

CITY: San Luis Obispo

FOOD: 4

SERVICE: 5

VENUE: 4

RATING: 4

WRITTEN REVIEW: Oki Momo is an Asian fusion restaurant, with an a-la-carte-style menu allowing you to sample multiple menu items for less than the cost of buying each individual item. The food is relatively cheap, and very good for the price. Much of the food is made in-house; the cashier began hand-making wontons when there were no customers waiting. The only complaint I had with the food was that some of the food wasn’t as strongly-flavored as most authentic asian food, but that’s likely done intentionally to appeal to a larger audience.

The service at Oki Momo is about as good as it can be for the type of restaurant. You order and pay up front, and the woman behind the counter was very friendly. The food was brought out very quickly, and we ordered a frozen desert that the waitress offered to hold on to for us, and deliver it when we were done with the main course. It’s the kind of place where you bus your own food, but the waitress offered to do that for us as well.

The venue is very nice and clean. It’s a small building decorated in a modern, minimalist style, in a slightly hard to find location. It uses the small space well, and the seating is very comfortable. The kitchen is visible from the seating area which looks nice, but the seats next to the kitchen are uncomfortably hot from the grill.

The biggest problem with Oki Momo is probably the lack of really strong flavors that can be reasonably expected from an asian restaurant. Even the extra sauces they provide to spice up your meal were milder than you’d expect. The size is also a problem; the largest table (except for the bar) only seats four people, so without pushing tables together you could never go there with a group larger than four. This could be a problem for many families, which would make Oki Momo less attractive.
