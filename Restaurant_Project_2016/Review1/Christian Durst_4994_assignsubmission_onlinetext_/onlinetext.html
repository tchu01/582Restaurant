REVIEWER: christian skylar durst


NAME: Indigo Moon


ADDRESS: 1980 Main Street


CITY: Cambria


FOOD: 5


SERVICE: 3


VENUE: 4


RATING: 4


WRITTEN REVIEW: Indigo Moon's menu is far more expensive than we normally go for, but we went anyway due to a special occasion. My spouse got a shrimp pasta while i got a ribeye steak; my first in years. Indigo Moon also has a special partnership with a cheese shop, so we got a fruit and cheese platter. The cheese platter included several novel cheeses and even fresh figs! The meal itself was fabulous: the steak was juicy and cooked just right and the shrimp were fresh. Our only complaint was that the bread that was served as a side to the pasta was overcooked and crusty.


My partner and i had just completed a backpacking trip, so we were a little grungy looking and under-dressed. This caused some immediate issues since we needed to change and freshen up in the bathroom; the hostess in particular was extremely dissatisfied with this. Once we changed into more formal clothes we were treated as serious customers, but i wish that we were treated more seriously from the start.


Indigo Moon is a quaint sit-down restaurant in the tourist-focused city of Cambria. So obviously it's used to serving customers who are looking to relax, try some new food and are unlikely to return for a while. Finding the venue was not too hard, but it's not very close to the beach and thus no ocean-view seating. It was also rather small, but it felt more personal and intimate rather than cramped.


Overall, it was a memorable meal for a special occasion for an atypical excursion. So while we won't be returning there ever again, it'll be a venue that my partner and i can remember with fondness.
